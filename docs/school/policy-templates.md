# Guardian School Policy Templates

**Ready-to-adapt policy language for schools deploying Guardian**

These templates provide schools with foundational policy language that can be customized to fit local context, requirements, and governance structures.

---

## Template 1: Guardian Deployment Policy

**For:** School board approval, administrative policy documentation

---

### [School Name] Guardian Online Safety Policy

**Policy Number:** [Insert]
**Effective Date:** [Insert]
**Review Date:** [Annual]
**Approved By:** [School Board/Leadership]

---

**I. PURPOSE**

This policy establishes guidelines for the deployment and operation of Guardian, an online predator detection system, at [School Name]. Guardian is designed to protect students from adult-initiated online harm while respecting student privacy and dignity.

---

**II. SCOPE**

This policy applies to:
- All students enrolled at [School Name] with parent/guardian consent
- School-owned devices
- [Optional: School network when students use personal devices]

---

**III. DEFINITIONS**

**Guardian:** Privacy-first software that detects adult predatory behavior patterns targeting students online.

**Safety Officer:** Designated school counselor responsible for receiving and responding to Guardian alerts.

**Alert:** Summary notification to Safety Officer when Guardian detects potential threat.

**Evidence:** Encrypted, sealed conversation records accessible only to parents/guardians.

---

**IV. GUARDIAN PRINCIPLES**

Guardian operates under the following non-negotiable principles:

1. **Protection, Not Surveillance**: Guardian detects threats only, not normal student activity
2. **Privacy-First**: Minimal data collection, local processing, automatic deletion
3. **Student Dignity**: Students are protected, not punished
4. **Parent Partnership**: Parents have primary access to evidence and decision-making
5. **Transparency**: Students and families informed of Guardian deployment
6. **Voluntary Participation**: Consent required, opt-out available

---

**V. WHAT GUARDIAN DETECTS**

Guardian detects behavior patterns associated with online predation:
- Adult grooming tactics
- Sexual solicitation
- Personal information extraction
- Isolation and secrecy tactics
- Meeting planning
- Coercion and threats

Guardian does NOT detect:
- Normal peer conversations
- Academic activity
- Screen time or device usage
- Location or browsing history
- Personal beliefs or expression

---

**VI. DATA PRIVACY & SECURITY**

**Data Collection:**
- Minimal: Only threat patterns, not full conversations
- Local processing prioritized
- Rolling buffer automatically deletes data
- Evidence created only when threats detected

**Data Access:**
- Parents/Guardians: Full encrypted evidence access (PIN-protected)
- Safety Officer: Summary alerts only
- Teachers/Staff: No access
- IT Personnel: System maintenance only, no alert access

**Data Retention:**
- Rolling buffer: Cleared continuously
- Evidence: 30-day automatic deletion (unless legally required)
- Alert summaries: 90-day retention
- Aggregate analytics: Anonymized, indefinite

**Data Security:**
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security audits
- Compliance with FERPA, COPPA, state privacy laws

---

**VII. ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES**

**School Safety Officer (Designated Counselor):**
- Receive and assess Guardian alerts
- Conduct student safety check-ins
- Notify parents of threats
- Coordinate intervention responses
- Maintain confidentiality
- Provide system feedback

**School Principal/Administration:**
- Ensure policy compliance
- Support Safety Officer resources
- Oversee Guardian deployment
- Approve crisis protocols
- Maintain parent communication

**IT Staff:**
- Install and maintain Guardian systems
- Troubleshoot technical issues
- Ensure data security
- No access to alerts or student data

**Teachers:**
- Understand Guardian purpose and limitations
- Refer student concerns to Safety Officer
- Support students appropriately
- No access to alerts or data

**Parents/Guardians:**
- Provide informed consent
- Access evidence when threats detected
- Collaborate on safety responses
- Support student wellbeing

---

**VIII. CONSENT & OPT-OUT**

**Consent Required:**
- Parent/guardian written consent
- Student age-appropriate assent
- Freely given, informed, specific

**Opt-Out Available:**
- Parents may decline consent
- Opt-out request honored within 48 hours
- No penalty or discrimination
- Re-enrollment available anytime

---

**IX. ALERT RESPONSE PROCEDURES**

**Critical Alerts (Imminent Danger):**
- Safety Officer response: <1 hour
- Immediate student safety check
- Parent notification: Same day
- Law enforcement consideration
- Documentation required

**High Alerts (Significant Concern):**
- Safety Officer response: Same day
- Student safety check: Within 4 hours
- Parent notification: Same day
- Intervention planning

**Medium/Low Alerts:**
- Response: 24-48 hours
- Educational intervention
- Parent notification if warranted

**See Safety Officer Playbook for detailed procedures.**

---

**X. PROHIBITED USES**

Guardian shall NOT be used for:
- Student surveillance or monitoring beyond threat detection
- Disciplinary enforcement
- Academic performance tracking
- Behavior management unrelated to safety
- Targeting specific demographic groups
- Any purpose beyond online predator protection

Violations may result in deployment termination and policy review.

---

**XI. MANDATORY REPORTING**

Guardian alerts do not automatically trigger mandatory reporting obligations. Safety Officers must assess each situation individually per state law and school policy.

Guardian evidence may support mandatory reports but does not replace professional judgment.

---

**XII. CRISIS RESPONSE INTEGRATION**

Guardian integrates with existing school safety protocols:
- Threat assessment team processes
- Suicide prevention procedures
- Law enforcement liaison
- Parent communication protocols

---

**XIII. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS**

**Safety Officer:** 45-minute Guardian training (annual refresh)
**School Leadership:** 30-minute overview
**Teachers/Staff:** 15-minute awareness module
**Parents:** Optional information session
**Students:** Age-appropriate introduction

---

**XIV. COMPLIANCE & OVERSIGHT**

**Compliance:**
- FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)
- COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)
- State/local privacy laws
- Guardian Charter principles

**Oversight:**
- Annual policy review
- Quarterly system audit
- Parent feedback mechanisms
- Student input opportunities

---

**XV. POLICY REVIEW & AMENDMENT**

This policy shall be reviewed annually and amended as necessary to:
- Reflect best practices
- Address community feedback
- Ensure compliance
- Maintain Guardian Charter alignment

## Template 3: Student Acceptable Use Policy Integration

**For:** Adding Guardian language to existing student technology policies

---

### Addition to [School Name] Student Acceptable Use Policy

**Section [X]: Online Safety & Guardian Protection**

**[School Name] is committed to your online safety. As part of that commitment, we use Guardian, a system that protects you from adults who try to harm students online.**

**What Guardian Does:**
- Detects when adults use dangerous tactics (grooming, solicitation, manipulation)
- Blocks harmful interactions
- Alerts our school counselor so you get support
- Protects your privacy by not monitoring normal activity

**What Guardian Does NOT Do:**
- Monitor your conversations with friends
- Track what you do online
- Read your schoolwork or messages
- Spy on you

**Your Privacy:**
Guardian respects your privacy. Your normal online activity is private. Only if an adult tries to harm you does Guardian create an alert.

**If Guardian Detects a Threat:**
1. Our school counselor will talk with you privately
2. Your parents will be notified
3. We'll work together to keep you safe
4. You won't be in trouble—you'll be protected

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Tell a trusted adult if someone online makes you uncomfortable
- Don't share personal information with people you only know online
- Report concerning behavior to [School Counselor Name]
- Ask questions if you don't understand Guardian

**Your Rights:**
- To know Guardian is active (we're telling you now)
- To have your privacy protected
- To ask questions about Guardian
- To talk to the counselor if you have concerns

**Questions?** Talk to [School Counselor Name] or [Administrator Name].

## Template 4: Staff Code of Conduct Addendum

**For:** Ensuring staff understand Guardian boundaries

---

### [School Name] Staff Code of Conduct: Guardian Provisions

**All staff must adhere to the following regarding Guardian:**

**1. Confidentiality:**
- Guardian alerts and student safety information are strictly confidential
- Discuss only on need-to-know basis with authorized personnel
- Never share details in public areas or with unauthorized individuals
- Violations may result in disciplinary action

**2. Access Restrictions:**
- Access Guardian data only if your role authorizes it
- Do not attempt to access alerts or student information beyond your authorization
- Report any unauthorized access attempts

**3. Prohibited Uses:**
- Do not use Guardian for discipline
- Do not use Guardian to monitor students beyond threat detection
- Do not pressure students to disclose Guardian-related information
- Do not discuss individual student alerts with parents (refer to Safety Officer)

**4. Appropriate Responses:**
- If student discloses concerning online interaction: Refer to Safety Officer immediately
- If you have questions about Guardian: Contact Safety Officer or Administration
- If you believe Guardian is being misused: Report to Administration

**5. Training Compliance:**
- Complete required Guardian training
- Attend annual refresher training
- Stay informed of policy updates

**I acknowledge that I have read and will comply with these Guardian provisions.**
